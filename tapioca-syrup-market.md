<p><p>The tapioca syrup market includes three main types: tapioca syrup, tapioca maltodextrins, and tapioca syrup solids. Tapioca syrup is a liquid sweetener derived from cassava, valued for its neutral flavor and high digestibility, making it ideal for a variety of food applications. Tapioca maltodextrins, produced through hydrolysis, serve as thickening agents and carriers in food and beverages. Tapioca syrup solids are dehydrated forms of syrup, commonly used in powdered products, offering sweetness and enhancing texture without adding moisture.</p></p>
