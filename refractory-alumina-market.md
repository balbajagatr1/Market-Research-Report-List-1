<p><p>The refractory alumina market encompasses several types, including calcined alumina, fused alumina, tabular alumina, and others. Calcined alumina is produced by heating alumina to remove water, enhancing its properties for various applications. Fused alumina, obtained through melting bauxite, offers high durability and abrasion resistance. Tabular alumina is created by sintering alumina at high temperatures, providing excellent thermal stability. Other forms may include reactive aluminas and specialty additives, catering to diverse industries such as ceramics, refractories, and metallurgy.</p></p>
