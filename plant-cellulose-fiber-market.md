<p><ul><li>Natural</li><li>Synthetic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The plant cellulose fiber market comprises two main types: natural and synthetic. Natural cellulose fibers are derived from plant sources, such as cotton, hemp, and bamboo, known for their biodegradability, breathability, and comfort, making them ideal for textiles and packaging. In contrast, synthetic cellulose fibers, like rayon and lyocell, are chemically processed from natural cellulose and offer enhanced durability, moisture-wicking properties, and versatility in applications. Both types cater to diverse industries, including fashion, home textiles, and non-woven products.</p></p>
