<p><p>Mobile robots in the logistics market primarily comprise Automated Guided Vehicles (AGVs) and Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs). AGVs are predefined route-following vehicles designed for repetitive tasks, utilizing guided pathways for navigation. In contrast, AMRs possess advanced sensors and AI capabilities, enabling them to navigate and adapt to dynamic environments without fixed routes. Both types enhance operational efficiency in warehouses and distribution centers by optimizing material handling, reducing labor costs, and improving safety and productivity within logistics operations.</p></p>
